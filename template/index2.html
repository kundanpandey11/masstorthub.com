Background Info

The Camp Lejeune water contamination incident stands as a tragic chapter in our nation’s history. For decades, thousands of military personnel, their families, and civilian employees stationed at Camp Lejeune were unknowingly exposed to toxic chemicals in the drinking water supply. The consequences of this contamination have been devastating, leading to numerous health issues and even loss of life.

The contamination at Camp Lejeune, which occurred between the 1950s and 1980s, resulted from the improper handling and disposal of hazardous substances. These pollutants seeped into the water supply, exposing millions of people to harmful toxins. It is a grave injustice that those who dedicated their lives to protecting our nation were subjected to such perilous conditions.

Who Can File A Camp Lejeune Water Contamination Lawsuit?

If you or a loved one has been affected by the water contamination at Camp Lejeune, you may be eligible to file a lawsuit and seek justice for the harm caused. The following individuals may have grounds to pursue a Camp Lejeune water contamination lawsuit:

Military Personnel: Active-duty, reserve, and retired members of the U.S. Marine Corps, Navy, Army, Air Force, and Coast Guard who were stationed at Camp Lejeune between the 1950s and 1980s.

Dependents: Spouses, children, and other family members who resided at Camp Lejeune during the specified period.

Civilian Employees: Civilian employees, including contractors, who worked at Camp Lejeune and were exposed to the contaminated water.

Surviving Family Members: In the unfortunate event of losing a loved one who served or resided at Camp Lejeune during the relevant timeframe and subsequently suffered health issues or passed away due to the water contamination, surviving family members may be eligible to file a wrongful death claim.

The water contamination at Camp Lejeune has been linked to various health conditions, including but not limited to:

  • Leukemia
  • Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
  • Bladder cancer
  • Kidney cancer
  • Liver cancer
  • Breast cancer
  • Lung cancer
  • Prostate cancer
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Birth defects in children born to exposed parents


If you or your loved ones have been diagnosed with any of these cancers or other associated health issues, it is crucial to explore your legal options. Filing a Camp Lejeune water contamination lawsuit not only seeks compensation for the physical, emotional, and financial damages you have endured but also aids in raising awareness and ensuring that the responsible parties are held accountable for their actions.
